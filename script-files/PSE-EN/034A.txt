Fifth period break…
We're moving to a lab for the next class.
But on my way there, I realize that I had forgotten my textbook in the classroom and hurry back.
*rattle*
Oh man, class is about to start...
Huh?
I didn't think anyone would be there, but someone is sitting at a desk.
She's resting her head in her hand with her arm propped up on the desk and gazing out the window.
"Class Rep?"
She's lost in thought, but when I walk over and say something, she snaps back to reality.
"<family>…
kun…"
*thud*
Right as she stands up,
her notebook falls from the desk to the floor.
"What are you doing here?
Class is starting soon.
We need to hurry to the lab."
I try to pick up her notebook for her.
"Oh!"
She yelps and reaches for the notebook.
However, before she can pick it up…
*flrrp*
The wind blows it open.
I can't believe my eyes for a moment.
There are insults written with colored pencils and markers.
It looks like a girl's handwriting, but it's rough.
"Wh-What's this about?"
"H-Hey, give that back!"
She snatches the notebook from me and hides it in her bag.
"Did those girls write that?"
I ask.
"…"
She lets out an exasperated sigh.
"Yep."
Her answer is quiet.
Her eyes...
are a little red.
"Class Rep,
were you...
crying?"
She bursts out laughing.
"Stupid,
like I'd cry over something so silly.
I'm just appalled that they'd waste their time with this."
"…"
Really, though?
She looked pretty sad when I walked in and caught her sitting alone.
"I always thought the people around here were childish, but I honestly didn't realize it was this bad."
"…"
"I can't get along with these people.
We can't even talk on the same level."
"Class Rep…"
"Oh, look at the time.
Class is gonna start soon."
She said while looking at the clock.
She puts away her textbook, documents, another notebook, and a pen case.
"Let's hurry, <family>-kun.
That teacher hates it when someone's late."
"Hey, Class Rep."
I give her a sincere look.
"Why are you looking at me like that?"
"Want me to say something to them?"
"What?"
"I can tell them to never do this again."
"…"
She slowly closes her eyes and sighs.
And then...
"I told you...

I don't want your help."
She explains.
"Why not?"
"You're meddling.
This is my problem, not yours."
"Why shouldn't it be my problem?
We're in the same class. I even sit next to you."
"…"
"You can't expect me to see something so awful and just do nothing."
The Class Rep gives me an annoyed look.
"So what, you want to act like a hero?
This is between me and them.
Stay out of it!"
She pushes her chair into her desk and turns to leave.
"You don't have to try and look tough.
It's better to have allies at times like these."
"What?!
　
And you're an ally?
　
Don't tell me… You think they're bullying me?!"
"Seems pretty obvious."
She looks down and chuckles.
"I was the one who picked on them first.
I got to them so bad that they're ganging up on me now.
The retaliation is awfully childish and weak, though."
"Still, there's three of them and one of you."
"There could be a hundred of those children and I still wouldn't be scared.
Anyway…"
She glares at me.
"Your unwanted, unneeded kindness bugs me way more!"
With that, she hurries away.
"Uh, hey!"
*rattle*
*slam*
She shuts the door behind her, leaving me alone in the classroom.
"I'm bugging her?"
I mutter at the door when...
*ding, dong, ding, dong*
The bell rings to signal the start of class.
"Oh crap."
I take my textbook out of the bag sitting on my desk and rush out of the classroom.
