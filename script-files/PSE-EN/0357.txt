She'll probably just ignore me or brush me off again.
But while I suspect as much, I still approach her to talk.
"Hey, Class Rep."
"<family>…
kun…"
She turns to me, her eyes as wary as usual.
"Want to walk with me?"
I smile, ready to be rejected.
And then...
"…"
After thinking for a bit...
"Sure."
She answers.
Then she starts walking.
"…"
The unexpected response leaves me standing there with my mouth agape.
"What's wrong?

Are you coming or not?"
"Y-Yeah, coming."
I nervously jog over to Class Rep's side and start walking down the hill with her.
What a turn of events.
I was sure she'd just ignore me again.
Man, this is weird.
Some days bring surprises, I guess.
I observe her face from the side as we walk. Something about her seems a little different than usual.
Her ever-icy expression seems somehow more warm and pleasant.
Maybe something good happened to her.
After walking about the length of a telephone pole, something else unusual happens.
"<family>-kun."
"Wah!"
Suddenly hearing my name automatically puts me on guard.
"What kinda reaction is that?"
"Oh, uh, ignore that.
Wh-What is it?"
"I just wanted to ask you something."
"What?"
Ask me something?
What could it be?
Well, whatever it is, it's very unusual for her to start a conversation with me.
"Um..."
Having some trouble getting the words out, she looks down at her feet.
"I don't know if this has anything to do with you, but…"
"Hm?"
"<family>-kun,
did you say something to those girls?"
She looks me in the eyes.
"…"
I knew what she meant right away.
It had to be about what happened between me and those girls in the classroom.
But still...
"Which girls?"
I play dumb.
"You know the three.
Okada and her gang from our class."
So, I was right.
What should I do?
If I'm honest, she'll probably complain about me meddling again.
Playing dumb seems like the better move here.
"What about them?"
I keep acting ignorant.
And then...
"Don't pretend. I can tell."
She laughs.
"I knew it.
You told them to say sorry, didn't you?"
She looks at me like she saw right through my lies.
"…"
There's no use trying to play dumb now.
Oh well, I surrender.
"Well, that's more or less what happened.
But how'd you know?"
"All three of them just came and said sorry about what they did to my notebook."
"Huh."
It didn't look like they felt especially bad about what they did when they left, but I guess they actually apologized.
Maybe they're more reasonable than their childish behavior lets on.
"Well, one of them didn't seem so on board, though."
Haha, that's almost certainly Okada.
She kept complaining to the very end.
"I can't imagine they'd say sorry on their own.
It had to be you or someone else who pushed them into it."
She lets out a deliberately loud sigh.
"I told you...
　
This was none of your business.
Why didn't you listen?"
"W-Well, what's the issue?"
I smile and try to justify myself.
"They probably felt bad enough to apologize either way.
It's not like I made them do it."
"…"
"They had to have at least felt kind of bad about the childish thing they did."
"Obviously."
She scoffs. I hear anger in her words.
"That's not something high schoolers do."
"Oh, Class Rep, are you mad at them?
Didn't you say you didn't care before?"
"I-I'm not really mad."
"Heheh."
I look away and try to stifle a laugh.
She isn't as prickly as she usually is today.
Having her problems with the three girls taken care of must have cheered her up.
She's letting her real feelings show. I feel like that proves she's less guarded now.
When observing her like this, her personality is surprisingly easy to figure out.
She tries to act cool, but she actually has intense emotions that she can't hide.
She seemed pretty mature to me until a moment ago, but now she comes across differently.
We aren't that different.
We are the same age, after all.
Realizing that makes me feel closer to her than before.
I can't help but smile.
"Hm?"
The Class Rep looks confused.
"Well, anyway, good for you.
Best to be on good terms with people."
"It's not like I'm their friend now."
She says, letting out a huff as she speaks.
"But at least I shouldn't have to deal with their nonsense anymore."
"Can you never be honest with yourself?"
"Did you just say something?"
"Don't worry about it."

"Hey, Class Rep,
you want to go somewhere with me today?"

Somewhere we can sit and get something to drink."

Time, huh?
Maybe I should've spent less time meandering around at school.

"Fine,
whatever."

"But this is the first time I've seen it."
"W-Well,
maybe I haven't smiled around you."
"Heh heh heh."

"I was just thinking that it was a long road to get here."

"But that smile was worth it."
