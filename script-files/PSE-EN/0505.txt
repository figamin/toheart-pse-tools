（葵の出逢いイントロより）
"Oh yeah, they're going to run out of pork cutlet sandwiches!"
I forgot what was really important.
I chase after Masashi.
But it's too late.
Students are gathered around the sandwich counter, and the pork cutlet sandwiches are already sold out.
My second choice, the sausage rolls, are gone too.
"You're a little late."
"Dammit!"
"What can you do?
We'll buy what's left when the crowd dies down."
"Like there's gonna be anything good left..."
Left with no other choice,
we wait to the side of the cafeteria until the wall of people thins out.
I wait around bored until…
"Hi!
<kata>!"
A cheerful voice calls to me.
"<kata>, you're not eating lunch?"
"Hey, Lemmy.
I got here late today,
so I just have to wait to get whatever's left for me."
"The last stuff left is never much good.
Let's eat the set lunch together."
"You mean the school lunch?
Well, I guess that could be alright sometimes."
"We've been having nothing but sandwiches for a while."
Masashi and I nod.
"Alright, Lemmy, want to eat lunch together?"
"Okay!"
"Now, what to eat?
Masashi, what are you getting?"
"A Lunch."
Masashi picks today's "A Lunch."
It's chicken katsu and salad.
"Oh, good pick.
I'll go for that too."
Masashi and I buy tickets for the A Lunch.
"And you, Lemmy?"
"I'm getting this!"
She pushes the button for tempura udon.
She's ordering a large serving too.
"Tempura udon, of all things?
And you're American?"
"So what? 
That's a stereotype.
I love udon."
"I guess some people would like udon, but..."
"It's not weird at all."
We sit at an open table and pick up our chopsticks.
"Now, let's eat!"
We dig in.
After taking a first bite, Lemmy suddenly grabs a pepper shaker.
She starts sprinkling it on her udon.
"H-Hey, Lemmy, pepper doesn't go on udon!"
"It's fine,
I like it!"
"Doesn't matter. As a Japanese person, I can't just let you…"
However, even as I speak, Lemmy's strange behavior continues.
Next, she grabs a bottle of okonomiyaki sauce and pours that on the tempura.
"Aaaah..."
Even Masashi is wide-eyed.
"Look, you can't do that."
"Aww, but it tastes good."
"Seriously?
The sauce is gonna mix with the udon broth."
"Bon appetit!"
However, Lemmy ignores us and starts chowing down on her creation.
Watching her enjoying it makes me feel silly for fretting over food like this.
Is it actually good?
"…"
Maybe I'll try it sometime.
It'd give me something to talk about.
