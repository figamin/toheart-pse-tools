"You sure are impressive, Aoi."
"Oh,

d-do you really think so?"
"You work hard every day from morning to night.
I really respect that."
Aoi smiles bashfully in response.
"I-I'm someone who generally doesn't have any talent, so I have to put in more effort than the average person.
That's why I…"
"No talent? That's definitely not true."
I cut her off.
"What?"
"You do have talents.
Not finding working hard a chore is a great talent in itself."
"The ability to work hard is a talent?"
"Yup!
Don't you remember what Confucius said?
That knowing it does not compare with loving it,
and loving it does not compare with delighting in it."
"Hm?"
"It means that you can't beat someone who loves what they do.
So you must have a talent for martial arts if you truly enjoy practicing it."
"I-Is that really so?"
"You enjoy martial arts, right?"
"Oh, yes."
Aoi gives a big nod.
"Then that's a bona fide talent of yours."
"Oh..."
"So you should have more confidence.
You're my teacher and all."
I give Aoi a pat on the shoulder and she drops her head bashfully before looking up at me.
"Yes, I think I am starting to feel more confident!

Thank you very much!"
She grins.
Practice continues until the evening that day.
As we're leaving, Aoi gives me a book about martial arts to borrow.
"This book covers many of the basics of martial arts.
I am sure you will pick up some things if you read it."
"Ooh, thanks. I'll give it a read."
I take the book, and Aoi beams with joy.
