"I don't believe you."
I say bluntly.
"Why not?!"
"You know what people say about you?
You're a walking gossip show, a tabloid in a school uniform, that kind of stuff."
"Hey..."
"Even if you were half-trustworthy, the correct half would be the boring stuff and all the interesting stories would be fake."
"How rude! That's not true!"
"Then what happened with that story?
The one about the senior teacher making moves on a student and getting fired?"
"What?
　
Oh, yeah, that."
"How about when you said the Board of Education was planning to demote our principal?"
"M-Maybe they decided to postpone that."
"And when you said one of the seniors was dating a famous musician?"
"I'm pretty sure they're still dating."
"Those were all lies."
I narrow my eyes.
"You can only cry wolf so many times before nobody believes you anymore."
"Why do you have to bring Grimms' Fairy Tales into this?"
"That one's Aesop."
Shiho clears her throat.
"S-So basically...

To survive in this advanced information society, receivers of that information need media literacy too.
You can't just take everything at face value."
"Pretty much.
That's why I'm not trusting you."
"Hey…"
"I can't put any faith in your nonsense. It'd be unfair to Class Rep."
"What the heck?!
　
Isn't that unfair to me?!"
"Why would it be?
Don't be ridiculous."
"Wh-Whatever, stupid!"
Shiho sticks out her tongue,
then turns away and runs down the hill.
Anyway...
Who knows if Shiho has her facts right,
but I just can't imagine that earnest class rep doing that sort of thing...
Hmmm...
Yeah, I don't think I should believe her.
