March 10th...
Morning already?
I should get up before Akari arrives.
"Good morning, <name>-chan."
"Hey."
"You seem tired."
"...Meh."
"Hehe,
<name>-chan, you've got some hair sticking up."
"What?!"
I frantically press my hair to my head.
"Where?"
"In the back."
I touch the back of my head.
"You're right.
Whoa, it just keeps springing up again."
It makes me feel like a manga character.
If I go to school like this, they'll give me some goofy nickname.
"Do you think you can fix it?"
"Doesn't seem like it.
This sucks.
A little water's not gonna do it, but I don't have time to wash my whole head."
"…"
"…"
We stand there thinking at the door.
"Oh, I have an idea."
"What's your plan?"
"Let me come in for a bit."
Akari takes off her shoes and enters the house.
"What are you looking for in the kitchen?"
"Do you have any towels I can wet?"
"Just use the one hanging there."
"Okay."
She soaks the towel with water.
"A wet towel's not gonna fix it."
"It'll be fine.
Now let me use the microwave."
Akari sticks the wet towel in the microwave.
"What's microwaving a towel gonna do?"
"Hehehe, just watch."
Akari sounds confident.
*beep*
"…"
The microwave finishes.
"Yeowch...
Here you are."
Akari holds the steamy towel out to me.
It's like the hot towels they give you at restaurants.
"I see, and that'll keep the hair down?"
"Yep."
Akari nods.
I place the towel against my hair right away.
"That's a neat trick you've got there.
Where'd you learn it?"
"I saw it on TV the other day."
"Huh, 
good to know."
