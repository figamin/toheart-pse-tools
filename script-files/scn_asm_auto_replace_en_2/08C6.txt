（レミィ　回数イベント　寄り道２　ＬＶ１）
（選択肢　話す）
「なんだ？」
"Do you know what country tempura came from?"
"Tempura? It's obviously from Japan."
As far as I know, tempura is one of the signature foods of Japan.
「残念ネ、<kata>。
テンプーラは日本が最初じゃないの」
Lemmy says, speaking in a self-assured tone.
"I've never heard of that before."
"Okay, so tempura originates from the Portuguese word 'temporas' or
'tempero' and the cooking style was brought over to Japan during
the 16th century by Portuguese Jesuits."
"And those other words just happened to turn into 'tempura'? Isn't
that a bit too convenient?"
"Someone named Santo Kyoden called it 'tempura' and that's how it
got its name."
"Who's that?"
"A writer."
"Don't know him."
"I don't know him either."
She's not really owning this story.
"Where'd you hear that from?"
"I read it in a book."
"For argument's sake, let's assume that's all true. I don't see the
advantage of finding out that tempura isn't a Japanese food."
"There are advantages."
"Like what?"
"While I'm eating tempura I can think to myself that what I'm eating
isn't actually a Japanese food."
"...Is that a good thing?"
「うん」
"It's not good at all."
"And it's fun to tell others that little bit of trivia."
"No, it's not."
"Oh, and you know what? Sushi isn't from Japan either."
"Yeah, yeah, okay. I don't care what country sushi is from."
「それでネ、それでネ──」
Lemmy makes me listen to a bunch of useless facts about the roots
of Japanese culture. I don't know if I gain anything from this
conversation or not.
I'm sure Lemmy is just looking for someone to talk to.
...Since she seems to have so much fun sharing her facts.
