（レミィ　回数イベント　寄り道１）
（書店）
While I am hanging around the shopping arcade on the way home, I
see someone familiar leave the bookstore.
Someone who always stands out from the crowd. It is...
"Look who's here. Hey, Lemmy."
Someone with blonde hair in a Japanese school uniform will always
stick out—whether we're out in town or at school.
"Oh! And just like that, a rice cake drops into my open mouth!"
Lemmy says something odd when she notices me.
And she seemed awfully happy about it, too.
"What in the world was that?"
"A Japanese saying."
"What happened to your mouth? Wouldn't you choke?"
"No, the saying goes, 'a rice cake drops into your open mouth.'"
"Who the heck has heard of that saying before? It's definitely not
Japanese."
"It's used when good things happen without any effort involved. It's
a fine Japanese saying."
"That's the first time I've ever heard that."
"I feel like the beautiful culture of Japan is gradually fading away..."
Lemmy mutters disappointedly, as if she is experiencing it herself.
"You should just leave that to the big brains to worry about."
The only thing I can say for sure is that I definitely don't have
the brains for it.
"It worries me..."
This conversation has taken a grim turn...
　オレは──。
A. Change the topic.
B. Leave.
