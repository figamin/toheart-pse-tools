（レミィとの出逢いイントロ）
（３月５日（水）夕）
（書店）
I am wandering around the shopping arcade without much thought.
As I'm about to stop by a book store to do some quick reading, I
notice a small boy crying in the corner of the store.
The boy of maybe five or six is bawling his eyes out.
People walk by him without even batting an eyelash.
"Geez..."
I approach the boy.
"What's wrong, kiddo?"
The girl seeks reassurance.
"Mommy... My mommy... *sniff*"
The child just keeps saying "mommy" and won't give me a straight
answer.
"...Did you lose your mom?"
This time, he nods as he continues to cry.
"I'll help you find her. What's the name?"
"*sniffle* Sachiko..."
「それはお母さんの名前だろ。
お前んだよ」
"...Takamitsu."
"Takamitsu, eh? That's a good name. How old are you?"
「ぐすん…５才」
"Five years old... So you'll be in grade school next year. You can't
be standing around crying just because you lost your mom."
"*sniff*"
His mom must be nearby if he got separated while they were shopping.
I look around but can't find her at all.
"I'm tired, Mister."
Takamitsu plops down on the floor.
We've been walking for an hour now...
The kid's still little, his legs must have reached their limit.
I chuckle.
"Guess I'll have to piggyback you then."
I crouch down and present my back to Takamitsu.
・

　母親を捜して、オレはタカミツをおぶって、狭くはない商店街を３時間近くも歩き回り、ようやく見つけた頃には、とっぷりと日が暮れていた。
Takamitsu's mom had apparently been looking for him outside the
shopping arcade.
I could have just taken him to a police station and saved myself
the hassle, but I wanted to make sure the kid found his mom safely.
　自分もガキの頃、同じ目にあった──ってのが、その理由かな。
　とにかく、その日はくたくたで、家に帰る頃にはすっかり足が棒になってしまった。
