（３月２０日　昼）
（自分の部屋）
Still, it's a day off. I should use it better.
Maybe I'll hit the town.
And I'll get something to eat while I'm out.
I decide to eat at a new rice bowl place I was hearing a lot about.
　中に入り、カウンターだけの細長い店内のひとつの席に座る。
There are quite a few customers.
"Hello! What can I get for you?"
A pleasant man greets me.
「カツ丼ひとつ」
"You got it!"
・
・
・
I waited five minutes.
"Here ya go!"
　目の前に、湯気の立つご飯と具が置かれた。
The pork katsu at this place is a bit atypical. The rice and the
toppings come separately.
There's a little bowl with a perfectly cooked runny egg.
Under that is a big piece of pork katsu.
I set it on the rice, then take a bite.
Huh?
I take a bite.
*munch, munch*
"Delicious!"
The pork is incredibly tender and well-seasoned throughout with the
flavor of dashi broth.
　それがまた、ご飯にあう。
"How'd you get it like this, Mister?"
I can't help but ask the man.
He's happy to tell me.
Apparently he uses tomato juice as a secret ingredient in the broth,
and the rice is a blend of Sasanishiki and Omi rice.
The pork is pounded to make it tender, then breaded with bread
crumbs made mostly from crust, and left to sit overnight.
I see. That explains why this tastes so good.
The cook's effort and ingenuity paid off.
I have no doubt that this place will start seeing some long lines.
It had been so long since I ate such great food that my spirits are
high for the rest of the day.
