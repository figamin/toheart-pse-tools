（レミィ　好感度イベント　ＰＳ）
Alright, I've got a date with Lemmy today.
I'm meeting her at the station at 10 o'clock, so I should hurry up
and get ready.
Unlike my usual rushed mornings, I take the time to make myself
look presentable.
I leave home with plenty of time to spare.
It is 9:45 when I get to the station.
And you, Lemmy?
「Ｈｉ！
　<kata>」
Someone suddenly calls out to me from behind.
"O-Okay..."
It is Lemmy, obviously.
"How you doin'?"
"You're pretty early."
I am captivated by Lemmy.
　ベージュ色のオーバーオールがレミィの元気なイメージにマッチしてて、いい感じだ。
Not to mention her attention-getting blonde hair.
Lemmy looks like she just walked off the page of a fashion magazine.
It'd be strange not to be captivated by her appearance.
「どうしたの？
　<kata>」
Lemmy says, linking arms with me.
She is always so forward, and it makes me self-conscious.
"N-Nothing. I'm just not used to seeing you in your regular clothes..."
Lemmy exclaims with joy and hops away from me.
"Does my outfit look good on me?"
She asks, slightly fishing for a compliment.
"It looks great! Perfect, even."
I give her a thumbs up.
"Thank you very much!"
Lemmy winks at me.
Normally, I'd be too embarrassed to have a back-and-forth like this,
but it feels so natural with Lemmy.
It is as if I have been thrown into an American TV show.
"Alright then."
"Hey, what's the bowling alley like?"
"The one we're going to?"
「うん」
I describe the bowling alley we are about to visit to Lemmy.
We are headed to a place that is known locally as a mecca for
bowling.
It was recently constructed with a gaming arcade and karaoke on the
first floor, and a bowling alley on the second floor.
There is also a small restaurant inside, so you can spend the whole
day there if you want to.
"We should have invited Shiho too."
"Nah, forget about her. Besides, we're on a date today."
"I will!"
"Okay, let's go!"
We climb the stairs and are greeted with a huge venue full of
bowling lanes.
It is still morning, so there are plenty of empty lanes.
"I'm super impressed..."
Lemmy's eyes practically fall out of her head. Maybe she has never
seen 50 lanes in one bowling alley before.
I go over to the counter and buy two games for us.
"Let's grab some bowling shoes."
「うん」
We pick up our rental shoes from the vending machine and then go
find some balls.
There is an array of different colored balls.
I settle on a 13-pounder to start with.
　レミィはというと、おもちゃ売り場の子供状態だ。
「レミィは女の子だから──」
"Okay, I'll take this one!"
She grabs a ball with the number '18' painted on it.
"You doofus! You're not gonna be able to bowl properly with a ball
that heavy!"
"Really?"
"Of all the balls, Lemmy! Don't pick the heaviest one."
An 18-pounder is rare. I have almost never seen anything above 15
at other places.
"Then... I'll take this one."
She has a 17-pounder in her arms.
"No, not that one either."
I shake my head, and Lemmy sheepishly returns the ball to the rack.
"Bye."
"Don't even think about the 16, 15, or 14 either."
I say for good measure, stopping Lemmy in her tracks as she reaches
for the 16-pounder.
"But bigger is better..."
"Not all the time."
"Oh woe is me..."
She looks deflated.
I am somewhat exasperated as I start to compare the size of her arm
to the balls.
「やっぱレミィは１１辺りで──
ストーップ」
Lemmy was already rushing over again but freezes when she hears my
voice.
「はい？」
"Don't pick up a six or an eight either."
「アハハッ、<kata>、勘がするどいネ」
"You don't need to be sharp to know what you're thinking when you
run to the end of the rack."
...From one extreme to another. Geez.
"But the smaller the easier to maneuver..."
"Lemmy, you're an 11. Grab an 11-pounder."
"Oh woe is me..."
"There's no woe for anyone."
I make Lemmy pick up an 11-pound ball as we go over to the benches
at our lane.
Now I have to explain to her how to bowl the ball.
"Hey, Lemmy? Do you seriously not know how to bowl a bowling ball?"
I ask for the hell of it.
Lemmy turns and rolls the ball around with a troubled look on her
face.
「<kata>、大変ッ」
"Of?"
"This ball only has three holes in it."
「…アホ」
・
・
・
I sit Lemmy down on the bench and demonstrate how to bowl the ball
in front of her.
「──って、だいたいこんな感じで投げるんだぞ。
まわりにオレより上手いヤツがごろごろしてると思うから、そいつの投げ方を参考にしてもいいけどよ」
　いちおう、オレ流の投げ方を説明してみた。
I don't know if my form is correct, so it is a pretty rough
explanation.
"This is more difficult than I expected..."
　教えてみると、手と足を同時に動かしたり、結構複雑なことをやってるのに気づいた。
Lemmy is right.
"Look, just give it a shot."
「うん」
I hand the ball over to Lemmy, and she puts her fingers into it,
ready to bowl.
"Start with your right foot."
She nods in response to my directions and steps forward with her
right foot.
Her footwork is fine, but then she suddenly swings her right hand
back.
"No, no. You're supposed to bring your right hand up in front of you."
"Whoops, I forgot."
"Try again from the start."
「うん」
Lemmy readies herself again.
This time, she brings her right hand up in front of her as she
steps with her right foot.
That's the way.
Then she steps with the left and again with the right...
"Stop. You've got the ball stuck in front of you."
"Why'd I do that? Ahaha! Silly me."
...She's more uncoordinated than I thought.
"Look, watch me again. On the first step, bring the ball up in front
of you. Second step—lower the ball. Third step—swing the ball back
and then throw it with your fourth step."
I demonstrate to her again.
「Ｏｈ、Ｉ　ｓｅｅ。
見きったヨ」
"Really?"
"Of course!"
Lemmy nods enthusiastically and then readies herself.
　一歩踏み出して、
二歩、
三歩…。
Yes! Looking good.
　いちおう形になってる。
　四歩で腕を振り下ろして──っこら！
"Don't throw the ball there!"
Lemmy actually lets the ball fly.
The ball rolls across the carpet and then into the bowling lane.
It careens diagonally into the lane and then straight into the
gutter, as expected.
"Bahaha! Gutter ball!"
"You shouldn't be laughing at that!"
That gutter ball is recorded as a roll in the first frame.
The letter 'G' appears in my scoreboard.
「<kata>、ｇｕｔｔｅｒだね！
Ahahaha."
Lemmy must have found it hilarious as she holds her stomach in
laughter.
"Gah! You did that on purpose, didn't you?"
"Sorry, my bad. I didn't mean to do it. I just suddenly felt like a
pro bowler so..."
Lemmy smiles sheepishly.
"Oh? So you're saying you accidentally let go, huh? Then let's see
what Pro Bowler Miyauchi's real skills are then!"
"Okay, I'll show you my chops!"
I am only teasing her, but Lemmy seems to take it pretty seriously.
She picks up her ball from the ball returner and stands in front of
the lane.
　精神集中する彼女は、結構マジだ。
Eventually, she slowly takes a step forward.
Now she really looks like a pro bowler!
*thud*
　手を離れたボールはきれいにレーンへ落ちた。
From the moment the ball leaves her hand to even after it begins to
roll, she looks like a pro.
It rolls with a diagonal spin to it, and is right on course.
　このまま行けば、１番ピンと３番ピンの間に──って、おいっ！
The silence is deafening.
Lemmy's ball rolls into a pinless oblivion.
"...Whoops, wrong lane."
She had bowled in an empty lane.
「アホ」
...And just like with her archery, Lemmy is great at looking the
part.
・
・
・
We play six games that day. My arm feels like it is going to fall
off.
Back and forth we go between the arcade on the first floor and the
bowling alley on the second floor. We spend almost half a day there.
Lemmy ends up improving and bowls fewer gutter balls.
My high score is 135 and hers is 83.
"That was fun."
「ああ」
「<kata>のおかげでアタシ、上手くなれたヨ」
"We both gotta get better at making spares."
"Okay. I hope you take me to the arcade again."
「そうだな」
"Okay, I'll see you later."
"Okay, see ya."
We part ways at the station.
Despite being the same age as me, Lemmy can be pretty childish
sometimes. I am never bored around her.
But that also makes me feel as though I have to keep an eye on her
and look after her...
I think to myself as I go home.
