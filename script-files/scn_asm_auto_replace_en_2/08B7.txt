I get up close to Lemmy and call out to her.
"Hey, Lemmy."
「あっ、<kata>！」
「帰んのか？」
「うん。
…<kata>も帰るの？」
「まあな」
「じゃあ、いっしょに帰ろ」
「いいぜ」
「<kata>、ニッポンのドラマって、どーして
ｌｏｖｅ　ｓｔｏｒｙばかりなの？」
Lemmy asks as we reach the hill.
「なんだよ急に」
Apparently, there's a popular TV series that's all the talk amongst
the girls in her class.
Because it has an all-star cast, is full of twists, and it always
generates conversations. It is the talk of all the classes.
What Lemmy finds curious is how every TV channel, every day is
filled with romantic TV shows and not necessarily the TV show in
question.
And when I think about it myself, she is right.
Lots of TV series air throughout the week, and most of them are
love stories.
"Do Japanese people like romance?"
"Hmm...
そう言われると、そうなのかも」
"So it is true."
"Did someone tell you that?"
"Yeah. Everyone in my class was talking about who they liked."
"Everyone as in all the girls?"
「うん」
"Girls do like talking about that kind of stuff..."
「じゃあ、<kata>」
「なんだ？」
「<kata>、好きな人いるの？」
"Another question out of the blue?"
"I got hit with a sudden wave of curiosity."
"You can say that again."
「お願い、<kata>。
いるかいないか、教えて」
Lemmy pleads with puppy-dog eyes.
When she does this, her gestures and demeanor are not different
from your average Japanese girl.
A bombshell blonde like someone from the movies acting like a
Japanese girl... There is a bit of a mismatch there.
But it is all part of what makes Lemmy so great.
