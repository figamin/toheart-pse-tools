（智子　日付イベント　１回目）
（４月９日　水曜日）
（必ず発生）
（１時間目の授業中　教室）
The first period of the day, in other words, the first class of the new school year, is homeroom.
Our new homeroom teacher is Mr. Kibayashi.
"Alright, first you all need to introduce yourselves.
Let's start from this side."
And so, the first thing I have to endure is the other kids' boring self-introductions.
Everyone lists off their name, previous class, after school clubs, and hobbies.
Still, there are over forty students in this class.
So, there's no shot that I'm remembering all of this.
Oh!
Looks like Masashi's up next.
"I'm Masashi Sato.
Last year I was in Class A.
I'm on the soccer team.
As for hobbies, uh, I like raising hamsters and stuff."
A few girls giggle.
"Hey, is he joking?"
"I'm not sure."
I hear them whisper.
Nah, it's no joke.
Masashi never jokes about himself to get laughs.
He's still got a smile plastered on his face, but I doubt he even knows what the laughs are about.
He's always been sort of oblivious in some ways.
Masashi does actually like to take care of hamsters and other such pets.
He's had pets like that consistently since kindergarten, if I remember right.
We always had him take care of the class pet in elementary school because of that.
As these thoughts passed through my head, my turn eventually came around.
What should I say?
A. "Hey, I'm <family> <name>. Let's make this year a good one, everybody." Take a friendly approach.
B. "Hey, I'm <family> <name>. Nice to meet you guys." Take a natural approach.
C. "I'm <family> <name>. It's a pleasure to meet you all." Take an inoffensive approach.
