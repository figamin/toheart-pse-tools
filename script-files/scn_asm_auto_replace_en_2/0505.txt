"Oh yeah, they're going to run out of pork cutlet sandwiches!"
I forgot what was really important.
I chase after Masashi.
But it's too late.
Students are gathered around the sandwich counter, and the pork
cutlet sandwiches are already sold out.
My second choice, the sausage rolls, are gone too.
"You're a little late."
"Dammit!"
"What can you do? We'll buy what's left when the crowd dies down."
"Like there's gonna be anything good left..."
Left with no other choice, we wait to the side of the cafeteria
until the wall of people thins out.

"Hi!
　<kata>！」

「<kata>、ランチ食べないの？」
"Hey, Lemmy.
今日は出遅れちまってな。
仕方なく残り物のパンを待ってんだ」
「残り物おいしくないよ。
みんなでいっしょにランチセット食べよ」
"You mean the school lunch? Well, I guess that could be alright
sometimes."
"We've been having nothing but sandwiches for a while."
Masashi and I nod.
「よしレミィ、いっしょにランチすっか」
"Okay!"
「さーて、なに食おうかなぁ？
　雅史、お前なに食うの？」
"A Lunch."

It's chicken katsu and salad.
"Oh, good pick. I'll go for that too."
Masashi and I buy tickets for the A Lunch.
"Is Lemmy here yet?"
"I'm getting this!"
She pushes the button for tempura udon.
She's ordering a large serving too.
"Tempura udon, of all things? And you're American?"
"So what? I love udon."
"I guess some people would like udon, but..."
"It's not weird at all."
We sit at an open table and pick up our chopsticks.
"Now, let's eat!"
We dig in.
After taking a first bite, Lemmy suddenly grabs a pepper shaker.
She starts sprinkling it on her udon.
"H-Hey, Lemmy, pepper doesn't go on udon!"
"It's fiiiine!
アタシ、こっちがスキ！」
「好きって、お前、さすがにそれは日本人として黙ってるわけには──」
However, even as I speak, Lemmy's strange behavior continues.
Next, she grabs a bottle of okonomiyaki sauce and pours that on the
tempura.
"Aaaah..."
Even Masashi is wide-eyed.
"Look, you can't do that."
"Aww, but it tastes good."
"Seriously? The sauce is gonna mix with the udon broth."
"Bon appetit!"
However, Lemmy ignores us and starts chowing down on her creation.
Watching her enjoying it makes me feel silly for fretting over food
like this.
